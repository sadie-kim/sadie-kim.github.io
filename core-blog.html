Emotional Intelligence

May 12

What is Emotional Intelligence?

It’s an ability to correctly recognise one’s own and other’s emotions and the ability to appropriately express and communicate emotions.

How is it different to IQ?

IQ is a short for ‘Intelligence Quotient’. It is a numerical value given to measure people’s intelligence level. IQ is innate, but EQ is acquired.

Why is it important?

EQ is important because a huge part of working especially in most industries is working in a team with a group of people. One’s EQ can translate to someone’s ability to work well with others in a team.

Exploring Neuroplasticity through having a Growth Mindset

May 12

Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity refers to the ability of our brain to continue growing when it is stimulated. Which is saying that our brain changes as we study and practice new skills. When people understand Neuroplasticity, they realise that they can achieve any learning goals with enough time and effort, as we have the control to change the brain as we like. Applying this principle in my own learning, it tells me that my goal of learning web development is an achievable goal as long as I put my mind to it. To increase my neuroplasticity in programming, I need to code everyday even for a short period of time.

Growth Mindset

Growth mindset is a viewpoint that anyone can learn anything with right learning methods based on neuroplasticity. This exploration didn’t surprise me this time, as we already went though this material in pre-foundation course. I am going to refer to this material every time I feel overwhelmed by the bootcamp.

Blend with your learning plan and strategies

The key takeaway from learning neuroblasts for me is that repetition is key. I need to set a daily routine so I can spend even a small amount of time in the things that are important to me such as coding or exercise.
